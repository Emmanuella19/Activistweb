Have you ever felt frustrated about the fact that feminine products like tampons, pads, period cups, etc. are priced even though women don't choose to bleed every month??!!

I mean, let's ignore the fact that this phenomenon is absurd for a second and instead ask ourselves why these feminine products are outlandishly expensive. Well, this is because of a little something known as thePink Tax. Kilpinger Personal Finance describes the pink tax as "state sales tax on menstrual products, like tampons and feminine pads" (Taylor).

The Pink Tax affects millions of women globally. A few examples of women who encounter this problem are:

- Women in the military
- Women in prison
- Homeless women
- Women in third-world countries
- Trans women
